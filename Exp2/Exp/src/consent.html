INDIANA UNIVERSITY STUDY INFORMATION SHEET FOR RESEARCH

Using cognitive models to understand learning strategies for real-world images:
OBJECT CATEGORY LEARNING: PROLIFIC

You are being asked to participate in a research study. Scientists do research to answer important questions that might help change or improve the way we do things in the future. This document will give you information about the study to help you decide whether you want to participate. Please read this form, and ask any questions you have, before agreeing to be in the study.

All research is voluntary. You can choose not to take part in this study. If you decide to participate, you can change your mind later and leave the study at any time. You will not be penalized or lose any benefits if you decide not to participate or choose to leave the study later.

The purpose of this study is to investigate how people learn to remember and categorize real-world visual objects.

We are asking you if you want to be in this study because you responded to an advertisement posted on the Prolific system. The study is being conducted by Dr. R.M. Nosofsky, Indiana University/Psychological and Brain Sciences. It is funded by the Australian Research Council.

If you agree to be in the study, you will do the following things. You will be learning to assign visual images into different categories, such as categories of faces, paintings, rocks, or everyday objects. We will test your memory for the learned categories and the visual images. The study takes place online via Prolific. It is a single session that will last about 45 minutes but less than one hour.

Before agreeing to participate, please consider the risks and potential benefits of taking part in this study. Except for a possible loss of confidentiality, there are no known risks in taking part in this study. There is a risk someone outside the study team could get access to your research information from this study. More information about how we will protect your information to reduce this risk is below.

We don't think you will have any personal benefits from taking part in this study, but we hope to learn things that will help researchers in the future.

You will be paid for participating in this study. You will earn $8 plus a possible bonus of $2 for good performance.

We will protect your information. All information that is collected from you is done anonymously. We do not collect your name or any identifying information.

If you have questions about the study or encounter a problem with the research, contact the researcher, Dr. R.M. Nosofsky 812-855-2534.

For questions about your rights as a research participant, to discuss problems, complaints, or concerns about a research study, or to obtain information or to offer input, please contact the IU Human Research Protection Program office at 800-696-2949 or at irb@iu.edu.

If you decide to participate in this study, you can change your mind and decide to leave the study at any time in the future.
